    Past Tense

    1. Simple Past (Preterite Tense)

    The simple past tense is used to describe actions that started and finished in the past. It is often used with time expressions like yesterday, last week, in 2024, an hour ago, etc.

    Structural formula: Subject + verb (2nd form) + object.

    Examples:

    • I played football yesterday.
    • I ate my lunch an hour ago.
    • She woke up early in the morning.
    • You didnâ€™t write a letter last week.
    • She spoke good English to the interviewer.

    2. Past Continuous

    The past continuous tense is used to describe an action that was happening in the past or a continuing action that was occurring.

    Structural formula: Subject + helping verb (was/were) + verb (ing) + object.

    Examples:

    • I was watching TV when you called.
    • She was reading a book while it was raining.
    • They were playing football all evening.

    3. Past Perfect Tense

    The past perfect tense is used to describe an action that occurred before another action in the past.

    Structural formula: Subject + had + verb (past participle) + object.

    Examples:

    • I had cooked the food before I ate it.
    • I had finished my homework before I went to bed.
    • She had packed her bags before the taxi arrived.

    4. Past Perfect Continuous

    The past perfect continuous tense is used to describe an action that was happening for a period of time before another past action or time.

    Structural formula: Subject + had been + verb (ing) + object.

    Examples:

    • I had been cooking for an hour before they arrived.
    • She had been studying all night before the exam started.
    • He had been working at the company for five years before he resigned.
